Overview

Written and directed by Quentin Tarantino, the movie follows the story of a young Afro-American slave by the name of Django who has been recently freed from the toils of slavery and taken under apprenticeship by a German bounty hunter, they surmise efforts together to eventually rescue his wife who has been bought by a brutal plantation owner in Mississipi.

Key Facts and Interesting Tidbits!

It's important to note that the movie is a work of fiction, but inspired by a real historical time period. This is set in 1858, an age in pre-Civil War South America notorious for the movement of slave trading. All characters are not related to any actual, real human persons or real events but inspired by their time sensitive elements.
Released in December 25th 2012, originally premiered in December 11 2012 in Ziegfield Theatre.
This is Quentin Tarantino's eight film.
Django Unchained shouldn't be confused as a sequel/relation to another western of a similar title 'Django', 1967.
Django Unchained won two oscars, 58 wins and 158 nominations in total, the most notable being an oscar winner for Best Original Screenplay.
The movie was so acclaimed, it had a seven volume graphic novel adaptation. Includes scenes from the original draft of D.U where Django gave the remaining inhabitants of the Candyland plantation a six versus one duel, Broomhilda's point of view after being sold off and more depth to Django being under Dr. Schultz wing.
A mistake in Django Unchained involves the several usages of dynamite onscreen, despite it being invented in 1867, 9 years after the time setting.
In the near end of the movie, Quentin Tarantino himself starred as a racist mining company transporter and gets blown up by said dynamite.
The genres for this movie are Western and Drama. Rated R for graphic violence, language and some nudity.
Django's lead was originally Will Smith, but the actor rejected. However, Quentin Tarantino met Jamie Foxx, and was instantly sold.

Characters

Django

D-J-A-N-G-O. The 'D' is silent.

The main character of the story, an afro-american former slave and presently a sharpshooting, skilled bounty hunter. And, in the words of his mentor 'The fastest gun in the South'. He's also the husband of Broomhilda Von Shaft, who was separated from her after being sold to different slavers for attempting to escape together. His full name, Django Freeman, a name he picked out as a statement of his freedom and unbridled spirit. With Schultz's guidance made his expert marksmanship and the man he is today. Eventually, they work together as partners to get his wife back.

Dr. King Schultz

Oh, and on the off chance that there's any astronomy aficionados amongst you, the North Star is THAT ONE. Tata.

A German ex-dentist pro bounty hunter who crossed roads with Django and freed him. An abolitionist from Düsseldorf who is of much culture as intelligence, previously having 5 years of dentistry to his name before becoming a bounty hunter in 1853. He befriends young Django later on, and become partners in a bounty hunter duoship, agreeing to help rescue his wife.
Random fact: During a horse riding training session, the actor for Dr. Schultz had fallen off a saddle whilst ontop a horse, breaking his pelvis.

Calvin Candie

Gentlemen, you had my curiosity, but now you have my attention.

The main antagonist of Django Unchained, a Francophile plantation owner of Candyland, the fourth largest cotton plantation in Mississipi-- inherited from his parted father. A wealthy, brutal and powerful man with a great deal of influence and is well within the slave business; apparently being the owner of Broomhilda.

Broomhilda Von Shaft

Hey Big Troublemaker.

Also known as her other name, Hildi, is Django's wife and came to be under Candyland's hold as a slave. Her name is derived of a German fairytale. Due to the fact that her former masters were German and taught her the language itself. She married Django under their ownership by the Brittle Brothers before being separated after their escape attempt.
